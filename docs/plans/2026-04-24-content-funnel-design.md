# Big Beach AL — Top-of-Funnel Content Strategy & Lead Pipeline Design

**Prepared:** April 24, 2026
**For:** Dave & Kelly — Big Beach AL
**Status:** Design complete (sections 1–8 validated). Implementation plan to follow via writing-plans skill.

**Companion doc:** [`2026-04-01-youtube-growth-strategy.md`](2026-04-01-youtube-growth-strategy.md) — competitor research, YouTube tactics, equipment, thumbnail/title formulas, 90-day playbook. **Read that for "how to win YouTube."** This doc answers a different question: **"How does YouTube (and organic content generally) plug into a two-track lead funnel that uses existing capture infrastructure and the CRM?"**

---

## The Problem We're Solving

Bottleneck identified: **top of funnel — sourcing.** Plenty of capture infrastructure (subdivision page, /quiz, condo search, CRM) but not enough strangers showing up at bigbeachal.com to feed it.

Paid acquisition was deprioritized (Zillow/Realtor.com bid wars are a knife fight). Vacation-rental partnerships were ruled out (rental managers stay neutral across all agents to protect referral flow from the broader agent base). **Organic content is the moat.**

---

## Section 1: The Structural Insight — Two Funnels, Not One

The production talent maps cleanly onto a two-track funnel, with each track terminating at a *different* existing capture endpoint:

| Track | On camera | Audience | Existing capture | CRM segment | Sales cycle |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Track 1: Relocation** | Kelly + Dave | Year-round movers, retirees, remote workers | **Subdivision page** | "Relocation lead" | 3–18 months |
| **Track 2: Vacation/Condo** | Kelly + Kerri | Second-home, investor, vacation buyers | **/quiz** | "Vacation/condo lead" | 30–90 days |
| **Track 3: Top-of-funnel amplifier** | Kelly solo (IG Reels/Stories) + lifestyle YouTube | Anyone curious about Gulf Shores | (routes to Track 1 or 2 based on content) | n/a until self-selects | n/a |

**Why two tracks instead of one generic funnel:**
- Audiences want totally different content (a retiree comparing Fort Morgan to Foley vs an investor pricing Phoenix West rental income)
- Sales cycles differ by an order of magnitude
- Follow-up cadence and language must differ
- Capture endpoints already exist for both — we're feeding existing infrastructure, not building new

Every video, blog post, and email belongs to a track. Track 3 (lifestyle/area attractions) is the funnel-agnostic "net" — it pulls in cold audiences and routes them to Track 1 or Track 2 via end-of-video CTAs based on which intent the content targets.

## Section 2: YouTube Architecture — One Channel, Smart Playlists

**Decision: single YouTube channel, multi-playlist organization.** Two channels would split subscriber/algorithm signal and double production overhead.

### Playlist structure

**Track 2 (Vacation/Condo) playlists:**
- *Buying a Vacation Home in Gulf Shores* — pillar content, "what to know before you buy"
- *Condo Tours* — building-by-building (Phoenix series, Lighthouse, Beach Club, Turquoise Place, Caribe, San Carlos, Crystal Tower, Crystal Shores, Seawind, Island Tower, Royal Palms, etc.)
- *Building Comparisons* — "Phoenix West vs Phoenix Orange Beach," "Beachfront vs lagoon-front," "Best buildings under $X"
- *Vacation Rental Income* — actual ROI walkthroughs by building

**Track 1 (Relocation) playlists:**
- *Relocating to Gulf Shores* — pillar content, "is Gulf Shores right for you year-round"
- *Neighborhood Tours* — driving tours of every subdivision (Craft Farms, Cypress Village, Bon Secour, Fort Morgan area, Foley, etc.)
- *Gulf Shores vs [Other Place]* — Destin, Orange Beach, Fairhope, PCB, Pensacola
- *Cost of Living & Practical Stuff* — taxes, insurance, hurricane prep, schools, healthcare

**Track 3 (shared / top-of-funnel) playlists:**
- *Area Attractions & Lifestyle* — restaurants, beaches, festivals, day trips
- *Gulf Shores Market Updates* — monthly market report (huge SEO; serves both audiences)
- *Meet the Team* — short intros, behind-the-scenes (trust building)

### Playlist mechanics
- **Track-matched CTAs.** Every video ends with a CTA matched to its track:
  - Track 1: *"If you're researching a year-round move, find your perfect subdivision — link below"*
  - Track 2: *"If you're looking at vacation condos, our quiz matches you to buildings — link below"*
  - Track 3: *"If you're dreaming about owning here, we have two free tools depending on whether you want a condo or a year-round home — links below"*
- Videos can be added to **multiple** playlists where it makes sense (a Market Update sits in both Track 1 and Track 2 playlists)
- Each playlist gets its own SEO-optimized title and description — playlists rank in Google too

## Section 3: The Atomic Content Model

The reason 95% of agent content engines die is that a "content week" balloons into 5 days of work and gets dropped. The fix:

**One shoot day → one full week of distribution per track.**

### Weekly production rhythm
- **Tuesday:** Kelly + Dave shoot one neighborhood/relocation video (~45 min in the car)
- **Thursday:** Kelly + Kerri shoot one condo/building tour (~60 min on site)
- **Editor (Kelly + Dave DIY in CapCut to start):** cut each into:
  1. The long YouTube video (10–15 min)
  2. Transcript → SEO blog post on bigbeachal.com
  3. 3–5 vertical clips → Reels / TikToks / YouTube Shorts (Kelly distributes through the week)
  4. Stills + pull quotes → Pinterest pins (highly underrated for retiree/lifestyle audience)
  5. Embed in matching CRM nurture step where relevant

**Net:** ~3 hours of capture time per week → ~15 distribution units across 5 channels → feeding 2 segmented funnels into the existing CRM.

### Track 3 (Kelly solo IG/Reels)
Kelly's existing daily Reels/Stories cadence continues unchanged — but every post must end with a CTA back to bigbeachal.com via the **bio splitter at `/start`** (replacing the current direct link to `/quiz`).

### A note on YouTube Shorts vs IG/TikTok shorts
The companion YouTube doc captures Andy Hollis's finding that YouTube Shorts attract low-intent viewers and were removed from his channel. Our position: **short-form lives on IG/TikTok/Pinterest** (Kelly's domain, optimized for discovery). On YouTube, short clips can be posted as Shorts but the channel's center of gravity is long-form.

## Section 5: Capture Mechanics

The job: turn a cold viewer/reader into a known lead in the CRM with the right segment tag.

### The 4 capture surfaces (ordered by viewer intent, hottest → coldest)

| # | Surface | Bait | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Existing capture** (subdivision page, /quiz) | The actual product — "find your subdivision" / "match me to condos" | Linked from every CTA |
| 2 | **Lead magnet PDF download** | Lower-friction trade for an email | Inline in blog posts, video descriptions, exit popups |
| 3 | **Bio splitter page** (`/start`) | Routes ambiguous traffic to whichever track they want | Kelly's IG bio link |
| 4 | **Sticky CTA / exit intent on bigbeachal.com** | Last-chance grab before they leave | Site-wide |

### Lead magnets (4 total — 2 per track)

**Track 1 (Relocation):**
- **The Gulf Shores Relocation Guide** — 20–30 page PDF: neighborhoods overview, cost of living vs national avg, taxes (AL has no state income tax — big hook for movers from IL/CA/NY), insurance reality, hurricane prep, schools, healthcare, year-round vs seasonal life. The flagship.
- **Cost of Living: [Your State] vs Gulf Shores** — short comparison sheets. Build versions for the highest-volume origin states (IL, OH, MI, NJ, CA, TX, GA). Each one reads "made for me" because it names the viewer's state.

**Track 2 (Vacation/Condo):**
- **Beach Condo Buyer's Cheat Sheet** — 8–12 pages: HOA red flags, rental restrictions by building, insurance breakdown, special assessments, the out-of-state buyer process.
- **Top 10 Gulf Shores Condo Buildings — Side-by-Side** — comparison matrix: price ranges, rental income estimates, beachfront vs not, family vs adult vibe, HOA cost, rental policy. Highly shareable.

### Bio splitter page at `bigbeachal.com/start`

Kelly's IG currently links directly to `/quiz`, which routes 100% of her audience to the condo funnel — losing every relocation lead. The splitter fixes that:

> **What brought you here?**
> - 🏠 *I'm thinking about moving to Gulf Shores* → routes to subdivision page
> - 🏖️ *I'm looking for a vacation condo or investment property* → routes to /quiz
> - 📖 *Just curious — send me your free guide* → routes to relocation guide download (lower commitment, captures email)
> - 📞 *I'm ready to talk to an agent* → routes to scheduling app

Three benefits over Linktree:
1. You own the page — full analytics, conversion testing, brand consistency
2. Segments the lead at the door — by the time they hit the CRM, they're already tagged Track 1 or Track 2
3. Every click is a bigbeachal.com pageview, not a Linktree pageview

### UTM scheme

```
utm_source    = youtube | instagram | pinterest | blog | tiktok | direct
utm_medium    = long-video | short | reel | story | pin | organic-search | bio-link
utm_campaign  = track1-relocation | track2-vacation | track3-lifestyle
utm_content   = <video-slug or post-slug>   ex: phoenix-west-tour
```

### Existing UTM handling — what's there and what's missing

`apps/api/views.py:48-127` already accepts `utm_source/medium/campaign/content` from form submissions, but **flattens them into a single `source_detail` string** on the Contact model. This works for capture but isn't queryable for reporting. Section 8 promotes them to **structured columns** so we can run real per-video, per-campaign attribution.

### Per-video CTA formula

Every YouTube video gets the same 3-part treatment, **CTA always matched to the video's track:**

1. **Pinned comment** with the track-matched link + UTMs (the second-most-clicked link on every video)
2. **Description, first two lines** with the same CTA + lead magnet offer
3. **End screen / verbal CTA in last 30 sec** — match to track, always say "links in description" out loud

### On-page mechanics at bigbeachal.com

- **Sticky bottom-of-screen CTA bar** site-wide — track-matched per page
- **Exit-intent popup** on blog posts — offer the matching lead magnet PDF (suppress if user already a known lead)
- **Inline lead-magnet boxes** in every blog post, mid-article and end-of-article
- **Embedded YouTube videos** in matching blog posts (helps YouTube watch time + keeps user on-site longer)

## Section 7: Measurement & 90-Day Milestones

Two principles before metrics:
1. **One north-star metric per funnel stage** — not 50 KPIs
2. **Manual weekly review now, dashboard later** — don't pre-build dashboards before knowing what matters

### The 4 north-star metrics

| Stage | North star | Where it lives | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Top-of-funnel** | Weekly new visitors to bigbeachal.com | Google Analytics | Friday review |
| **Capture** | New leads/week, split by track | CRM (filter by source/utm_campaign) | Friday review |
| **Nurture** | Reply rate to drip emails | CRM (EmailLog) | Friday review |
| **Conversion** | Conversations booked/week per track | CRM (scheduling app) | Friday review |

**Why these four:**
- Visitors is the only top-of-funnel number that matters — subs/views/followers are vanity until they land on your site
- Lead split by track tells you whether the two-funnel structure is drawing both audiences
- **Replies**, not opens or clicks. Replies are the single most predictive nurture metric
- Conversations per week is the leading indicator of closes 60–90 days out

### Secondary metrics (monthly review, not weekly)

- YouTube: subscribers, monthly views, average view duration, thumbnail CTR (target 5%+)
- Per-video lead attribution (UTM-tagged) — drives content roadmap
- Bio splitter CTR by route — diagnoses Kelly's IG audience intent
- Drip step-by-step open and click rates — shows where to rewrite emails
- Sales-cycle time per track — sanity check assumptions
- Lead → conversation → tour → contract ratios (only meaningful after 90 days)

### 90-day milestone targets

Calibrated for 2x/week posting cadence (one per track). Conservative.

| Metric | 30 days | 60 days | 90 days |
|---|---|---|---|
| Videos published (Track 1) | 4 | 8 | 12 |
| Videos published (Track 2) | 4 | 8 | 12 |
| Lead magnets live | 2 | 4 | 4 |
| YouTube subscribers | 50–150 | 200–500 | 500–1,200 |
| Weekly site visitors (organic) | 50–200 | 200–600 | 600–1,500 |
| Track 1 leads captured (cumulative) | 5–15 | 20–50 | 50–120 |
| Track 2 leads captured (cumulative) | 5–15 | 20–50 | 50–120 |
| Drip reply rate (any reply) | n/a | 5%+ | 8%+ |
| Conversations booked from cold leads | 0–2 | 3–8 | 8–20 |
| First closes from YouTube-sourced leads | Almost certainly 0 | Possibly 0–1 | 0–3 |

**Reality calibration:** Andy Hollis went 4 months with zero leads before traction. The bar in the first 90 days is **content shipped + leads captured + replies starting** — leading indicators, not lagging.

### Day-60 health check

If by day 60 these are all true, the machine is working — closes will follow:
- Content is shipping every week (no "we'll double up next week")
- Replies are starting (even 1–2 real conversations from a 30-lead base)
- At least one video accumulating Google search views (SEO kicking in)
- Bio splitter showing route choices (Kelly's IG audience finding the funnel)

If three of those four are missing at day 60, something structural is wrong. Re-look.

### Friday weekly review — 15 minute ritual (Dave)

1. Google Analytics → write down weekly visitors
2. CRM → filter leads created this week, split by track → write down leads/week per track
3. EmailLog → check replies received this week → write down reply count
4. Scheduling/tasks → count new conversations booked this week per track
5. Eyeball: is the YouTube channel up to cadence (2 videos this week)?
6. One sentence note: what changed this week, what to try next week

Keep in a single running doc. Multi-week history > any single week's numbers.
